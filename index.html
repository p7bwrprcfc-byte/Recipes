College Park Café-Style Lentil Salad

1990s copycat inspired by a slightly sweet, oil-and-vinegar deli lentil salad from Edgewater Drive in Orlando.

Yield
6–8 servings
Prep
20 minutes
Cook
25 minutes
Chill
Overnight

Ingredients

Lentils

  • 1 cup French green lentils (Le Puy) or regular brown lentils
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Water for cooking

Vegetables

  • 1 large carrot, peeled and finely diced (about ⅛-inch cubes)
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced
  • 3 green onions, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped

Dressing

  • ⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 garlic clove, finely grated
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon celery seed (optional but recommended)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the lentils.
  2. Place lentils in a saucepan with the bay leaf and cover with water by about 2 inches.
  3. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer 20–25 minutes until tender but not mushy.
  4. Drain well and discard the bay leaf.
  5. While the lentils are still warm, whisk together all dressing ingredients.
  6. Pour the dressing over the warm lentils and toss thoroughly.
  7. Add the diced carrot, celery, green onions, and parsley. Toss again until evenly coated.
  8. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  9. Before serving, taste and adjust seasoning. Add a splash of red wine vinegar if desired.

Notes

  • This salad is best after 24 hours.
  • It will keep in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
  • Do not overcook the lentils — they should hold their shape.
  • Dressing the lentils while they’re warm is the key to getting the classic deli flavor.
Future tweaks: Add 1 teaspoon honey for a sweeter finish, increase sugar to 1½ tablespoons if needed, add finely diced red bell pepper for crunch, or stir in chopped capers for a briny note.
